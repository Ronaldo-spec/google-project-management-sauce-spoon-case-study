## Executive Summary

This report summarizes a project management case study based on the Sauce & Spoon menu tablet rollout scenario from Google Project Management coursework. The case study focuses on applying project management concepts in a real-world business context, including project initiation, planning, stakeholder management, quality evaluation, retrospective review, stakeholder communication, and project closeout.

Sauce & Spoon is a small restaurant chain that wants to support growth and expansion by testing menu tablets at selected restaurant locations. The pilot is expected to help the restaurant serve more guests in less time, reduce wait time, improve order accuracy, collect useful operational data, and support future rollout decisions.

---

## 1. Case Study Context

Sauce & Spoon decided to run a pilot project to test the impact of installing menu tablets at restaurant tables. The tablet menu is expected to help the restaurant improve operational efficiency and collect useful data for future business decisions.

The project follows the work of a project manager responsible for reviewing project documents, clarifying goals, aligning stakeholders, identifying tasks and milestones, evaluating quality standards, and communicating project outcomes.

---

## 2. Project Objectives

The project supports several business and operational objectives:

- Reduce table turnover time.
- Reduce guest wait time.
- Improve order accuracy.
- Reduce negative customer reviews.
- Train staff on the new tablet workflow.
- Support future business expansion decisions with operational data.

---

## 3. Project Charter

The project charter activity focused on reviewing company documents, project proposals, meeting transcripts, and stakeholder communication to document key information.

The project charter covered:

- Project summary
- Project goals
- Deliverables
- Scope and exclusions
- Benefits and costs
- Attachments and supporting references
- Stakeholder misalignment and resolution notes

Key information identified during the charter stage included selected rollout locations, product mix improvement goals, table turnover reduction goals, employee training needs, customer review concerns, and data tracking requirements.

---

## 4. Stakeholder Analysis

The stakeholder analysis activity focused on evaluating stakeholders based on their level of power, interest, and influence over the project.

The purpose of this activity was to determine how the project manager should interact with each stakeholder, manage expectations, negotiate scope, and communicate progress.

The case also included a coalition-building activity where selected stakeholders were asked to support limiting the tablet rollout to the original project scope, instead of expanding it beyond the agreed pilot area.

---

## 5. Project Plan

The project plan activity focused on identifying project tasks, organizing them, estimating duration, identifying milestones, and documenting confidence levels.

Examples of project tasks included:

- Coordinate branding and customization of the tablet menu interface.
- Gather and evaluate stakeholder requirements.
- Research and select a suitable tablet vendor.
- Create a statement of work and vendor agreement.
- Install and test Wi-Fi connection readiness.
- Create training material for tablet menu usage.
- Prepare troubleshooting and technical support plans.
- Conduct tablet system testing.
- Create a change management plan for staff.
- Prepare standard operating procedures for cleaning and maintaining tablets.
- Conduct staff socialization meetings.
- Train key staff members as trainers.
- Schedule training sessions across locations.
- Run a full service simulation using tablets.

---

## 6. Quality and Evaluation

The quality and evaluation stage focused on defining measurable quality standards and developing evaluation questions for the tablet pilot.

### Quality Standards

| Quality Area | Target |
|---|---|
| Average ticket time - appetizer | 8 minutes |
| Average ticket time - entree | 12-15 minutes |
| Payment time | 1 minute or less |
| Order accuracy | 98% |
| Weekly technical issue reports | Less than 5% of customers |
| Lobby wait time | Less than 10 minutes |

The case also included customer survey planning and test launch evaluation. The survey was intended to measure customer satisfaction and identify what worked well and what needed improvement during the tablet pilot.

---

## 7. Retrospective Review

After the test launch, the project team reviewed customer survey results and conducted a retrospective meeting.

The purpose of the retrospective was to identify:

- What went well during the test launch.
- What needs improvement for future rollout.
- Lessons learned from staff training and tablet usage.
- Recommendations for the next implementation phase.

This activity demonstrated the value of continuous improvement and structured project learning.

---

## 8. Stakeholder Communication

The case study included communication with senior stakeholders regarding a tablet contract update that affected the project budget.

The communication task required explaining the contract change, describing the budget impact, and requesting executive guidance or approval. This demonstrates the importance of clear, concise, and context-aware stakeholder communication.

---

## 9. Project Closeout

The project closeout activity focused on summarizing project goals, implementation approach, and key achievements.

The closeout report was important because Sauce & Spoon hired another project manager to support future tablet rollouts at other locations. A clear closeout report helps transfer knowledge and provides a reference for future implementation.
